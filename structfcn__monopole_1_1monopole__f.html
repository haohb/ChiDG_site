Detailed Description

Gaussian function.

Three 1D Gaussian functions are computed; one for each dimension. They are multiplied together to create a 3D version of the function.

\( f_x(t,\vec{x}) = a e^{- \frac{(x-b_x)^2}{2c^2} } \\ f_y(t,\vec{x}) = a e^{- \frac{(y-b_y)^2}{2c^2} } \\ f_z(t,\vec{x}) = a e^{- \frac{(z-b_z)^2}{2c^2} } \\ f(t,\vec{x}) = f_x * f_y * f_z \)

Function parameters:

\( a - \text{Amplitude of the distribution} \\ b_i - \text{Offset in coordinate 'i'} \\ c - \text{Width of the distribution} \)
